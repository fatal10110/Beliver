# Content Production

## Art Direction

### Visual Style

**Overall Aesthetic:**
- Historical realism with subtle fantastical elements
- Medieval Middle Eastern / Mediterranean inspiration
- Grounded, earthy color palettes (sandstone, terracotta, olive, azure)
- Architectural authenticity adapted to fantasy setting
- Avoid overtly supernatural visuals (keep it low-fantasy)

**Reference Inspirations:**
- Crusader Kings III (character and UI design)
- Civilization VI (map and resource iconography)
- Total War series (military units and formations)
- Historical manuscripts and illuminations

### Art Asset Breakdown

#### 1. Map & Environment

**Terrain Types (8-10 Distinct):**
- Desert (sand dunes, oases)
- Farmland (irrigated fields, green valleys)
- Mountains (rocky peaks, passes)
- Forest (olive groves, cedar forests)
- Coastal (beaches, cliffs, harbors)
- Holy sites (special terrain with sacred architecture)
- Rivers and water bodies
- Urban areas (cities, villages)

**Asset Requirements:**
- Tileable terrain textures (4K resolution)
- Terrain transition blending
- Vegetation props (trees, bushes, grass) - 20-30 unique
- Resource indicators (farms, forests, quarries) - 15-20 unique
- Roads and pathways (stone, dirt variants)

**Production Estimate:**
- 2-3 Environment Artists
- Timeline: 3-4 months for full set
- Tools: Blender, Substance Painter, Unity Terrain Tools

#### 2. Buildings & Structures

**Per Faction (3 Factions × 15-20 Buildings = 45-60 Unique Buildings):**

**Common Buildings (Shared Across Factions with Minor Visual Variants):**
- Houses (3 tiers)
- Farms (3 tiers)
- Walls/Fortifications (3 tiers)
- Markets
- Granaries

**Faction-Specific Buildings:**

**Yahud:**
- Torah House (2 variants)
- Synagogue (3 tiers: small, medium, grand)
- House of Study
- Sacred Wall
- Archives (for scrolls)

**Kristan:**
- Mission House (2 variants)
- Chapel / Cathedral (3 tiers: small, medium, grand)
- Hospice
- Monastery
- Bell Tower

**Issam:**
- Madrasa (2 variants)
- Mosque (3 tiers: small, medium, grand)
- Caravanserai
- Minaret
- Library (Bait al-Hikma style)

**Visual Differentiation:**
- Yahud: Stone construction, Star of David motifs, rectangular designs
- Kristan: Cross symbolism, domed roofs, arched windows, bell towers
- Issam: Crescent and geometric patterns, minarets, courtyards, intricate tile work

**Asset Requirements:**
- 3D models (optimized for real-time rendering)
- Multiple LODs (Level of Detail) for performance
- Construction states (0%, 25%, 50%, 75%, 100% complete)
- Damage states (for sieges)

**Production Estimate:**
- 3-4 3D Artists
- Timeline: 4-6 months for all buildings
- Tools: Blender, 3ds Max, Substance Painter

#### 3. Military Units

**Unit Categories × 3 Factions = 30-40 Unique Unit Models:**

**Common Units (Visual Variants Per Faction):**
- Infantry (spearmen, swordsmen) - 3 factions × 2 types = 6 models
- Cavalry (light, heavy) - 3 × 2 = 6 models
- Ranged (archers, slingers) - 3 × 2 = 6 models
- Siege (rams, catapults) - shared models with faction banners = 3 models

**Faction-Unique Units:**
- Yahud: Temple Guardian, Lawkeeper (2 models)
- Kristan: Missionary, Crusader, Pilgrim (3 models)
- Issam: Mujahedeen, Scholar, Qadi (3 models)

**Visual Requirements:**
- Rigged and animated (walk, idle, attack, death cycles)
- Armor and clothing variations (3-5 per unit type)
- Faction colors and banners
- Weapon variations

**Animation Needs:**
- 8-10 core animations per unit type (idle, walk, run, attack, defend, death, celebrate)
- Total: ~300-400 animation clips

**Production Estimate:**
- 2-3 Character Artists
- 2 Animators
- Timeline: 5-7 months for all units
- Tools: Blender, Maya, Motion Capture (optional for realistic movement)

#### 4. UI & HUD

**Core UI Screens:**
- Main Menu
- Map Screen (primary gameplay view)
- Resource Management Panel
- Diplomacy Screen
- Combat Results
- Turn Report
- Store / Cosmetics Shop
- Player Profile / Leaderboards
- Settings

**UI Elements:**
- Resource icons (Faith, Food, Wood, Population, etc.)
- Building icons (60+ unique)
- Unit icons (40+ unique)
- Spell/ability icons (15-20 per faction = 45-60 total)
- Status indicators (happiness, devotion, trade routes)
- Notification badges
- Buttons, borders, frames (faction-themed variants)

**Visual Style:**
- Skeuomorphic elements (parchment, leather, wood textures)
- Faction color schemes:
  - Yahud: Blue and white, gold accents
  - Kristan: Red and gold, white accents
  - Issam: Green and gold, black accents
- Clear hierarchy and readability (crucial for strategy game)

**Production Estimate:**
- 2 UI/UX Artists
- 1 UI/UX Designer
- Timeline: 3-4 months for full UI suite
- Tools: Figma (design), Photoshop, Illustrator, Unity UI Toolkit

#### 5. VFX (Visual Effects)

**Spell Effects (45-60 Unique Effects):**
- Yahud spells: Fire from sky, divine shields, prophetic visions (glowing/ethereal)
- Kristan spells: Healing light, divine retribution (holy energy), sermons (aura effects)
- Issam spells: Sandstorms, plague effects, swift winds (speed lines)

**Combat Effects:**
- Sword clashes, arrow impacts
- Siege weapon projectiles and impacts
- Building destruction (rubble, fire, smoke)
- Unit death effects (fade out, collapse)

**Environmental Effects:**
- Day/night cycle transitions (optional)
- Weather (rain, sandstorms) - mood and strategic impact
- Seasonal changes (visual only, or tied to gameplay)

**UI Effects:**
- Button hover/click animations
- Notification pops
- Resource gain/loss indicators
- Victory/defeat cinematics

**Production Estimate:**
- 1-2 VFX Artists
- Timeline: 3-4 months
- Tools: Unity VFX Graph, Particle systems, After Effects (for pre-rendered cutscenes)

#### 6. Cinematics & Trailers

**Pre-rendered Cutscenes:**
- Opening cinematic (3-5 minutes): Tells story of Abrim and his three sons
- Faction intro cinematics (1-2 minutes each) - shown when selecting faction
- Victory/defeat sequences (30-60 seconds)

**Marketing Trailers:**
- Announcement trailer (2-3 minutes)
- Gameplay trailer (1-2 minutes)
- Launch trailer (1-2 minutes)
- Faction spotlight trailers (1 minute each × 3)

**Production Estimate:**
- 1-2 Cinematic Artists
- External studio for high-quality opening cinematic (optional, $50K-$150K)
- Timeline: 2-3 months for in-house cutscenes
- Tools: Blender, Maya, After Effects, Premiere Pro

## Audio Design

### Music

**Soundtrack Needs:**
- Main theme (epic, memorable, 3-4 minute piece)
- Faction themes (3 unique tracks, 2-3 minutes each):
  - Yahud: Traditional Middle Eastern instrumentation (oud, ney, strings)
  - Kristan: Gregorian chant influences, organ, choir
  - Issam: Arabic maqam scales, qanun, percussion
- Gameplay music (ambient, loopable):
  - Peaceful (4-5 tracks, 3-5 minutes each)
  - Tension (3-4 tracks for diplomatic crises)
  - Battle (3-4 tracks for combat turns)
- UI music (menu theme, victory/defeat stingers)

**Total Music:** 30-40 minutes of original score

**Production Estimate:**
- 1 Composer (or licensed library music + custom pieces)
- Timeline: 3-4 months
- Budget: $10K-$30K for original score, or $5K-$10K for licensed + custom hybrid
- Tools: Logic Pro, FL Studio, live recording (optional for authenticity)

### Sound Effects

**Categories:**

**UI Sounds:**
- Button clicks, hovers
- Notification pings
- Resource gain/loss sounds (coin clink for currency, chime for Faith)
- Turn completion fanfare

**Gameplay Sounds:**
- Building construction (hammering, sawing)
- Unit footsteps (infantry marching, cavalry galloping)
- Combat (swords clashing, arrows flying, siege impacts)
- Spell casting (ethereal whooshes, divine booms, mystical chimes)
- Environmental ambience (wind, birds, market chatter)

**Total Sound Effects:** 200-300 unique sounds

**Production Estimate:**
- 1 Sound Designer
- Timeline: 2-3 months
- Budget: $5K-$15K (mix of library SFX + custom Foley)
- Tools: Pro Tools, Audition, Foley recording equipment

### Voice Acting

**Scope:**
- Character voices for campaign missions (5-10 characters)
- Unit acknowledgments ("Yes, my lord!", "Moving out")
- Narrator for tutorials and cinematics (1 voice actor)
- Optional: Factional voice packs for units

**Lines:**
- Campaign: 500-1000 lines (if fully voiced)
- Unit barks: 50-100 lines per faction × 3 = 150-300 lines
- Narrator: 100-200 lines

**Production Estimate:**
- 5-10 Voice Actors
- Timeline: 1-2 months (recording + post-production)
- Budget: $10K-$30K depending on scope and actor rates
- Tools: Pro Tools, Audacity, professional recording studio

## Narrative Content

### Lore Development

**Core Lore Documents:**
- Full biography of Abrim the Wise (5-10 pages)
- Detailed history of each brother (Yahud, Kristan, Issam) and their followers (10-15 pages each)
- Sacred texts excerpts (fictional scriptures for flavor)
- Timeline of major historical events in game world (100-500 years of history)
- Geography and world-building (nations, cities, sacred sites)

**Delivery Formats:**
- In-game codex (unlockable lore entries)
- Flavor text on buildings, units, spells
- Campaign mission briefings and dialogue
- External companion website or wiki (community-buildable)

**Production Estimate:**
- 1 Narrative Designer / Writer
- Timeline: 2-3 months
- External consultant: Religious scholar for cultural sensitivity review ($3K-$5K)

### Quest & Event Writing

**Random Events (30-50 Unique Events):**
- "A prophet has arisen in your lands, proclaiming visions. Do you embrace or exile them?" (choice-based outcomes)
- "Plague strikes your capital. Food production drops 30% for 3 turns."
- "A bountiful harvest! Gain bonus Food this turn."
- "Merchants from distant lands offer exotic trade goods."

**Campaign Missions (10-15 Scenarios):**
- Each mission has:
  - Story briefing (200-500 words)
  - Objectives (3-5 per mission)
  - Victory and defeat conditions
  - Post-mission narration

**Dialogue (For Campaign):**
- 50-100 dialogue exchanges between characters
- Branching choices where applicable (3-5 key decision points)

**Production Estimate:**
- 1 Narrative Designer
- Timeline: 2-3 months
- Tools: Dialogue tree software (Yarn Spinner, Twine for prototyping), Google Docs

## Localization

### Target Languages (Phase 1)

**Launch:**
- English (US/UK)
- Spanish (Latin America + Spain)
- French
- German
- Portuguese (Brazil)

**Post-Launch (Within 6 Months):**
- Arabic (critical for theme)
- Russian
- Simplified Chinese
- Japanese
- Korean

### Translation Scope

**Text Volume:**
- UI text: 5,000-8,000 words
- Game content (descriptions, tooltips): 15,000-25,000 words
- Campaign and lore: 10,000-20,000 words
- Total per language: ~40,000-50,000 words

**Cultural Adaptation:**
- Religious terminology requires sensitivity (e.g., parallel faiths to real religions)
- Consult with native speakers and cultural experts per region
- Avoid direct religious translations; maintain fictional framing

**Production Estimate:**
- Professional translation agency ($0.10-$0.20 per word)
- Budget per language: $4K-$10K
- Total for 5 launch languages: $20K-$50K
- Timeline: 1-2 months per language (can be parallel)

### Voice Localization (Optional)

- Fully voice act major languages (English, Spanish, French, German)
- Budget: $20K-$50K per language for full VO
- Timeline: +2-3 months

## Asset Pipeline

### Production Workflow

**Concept Phase:**
1. Concept artists create sketches and mood boards
2. Art director approves designs
3. Feedback loop until design locked

**Production Phase:**
1. 3D artists create models based on concepts
2. Texturing and shading
3. Rigging (if animated asset)
4. Animation (if applicable)
5. Integration into Unity engine
6. Lighting and VFX pass

**Review Phase:**
1. QA tests visual quality and performance
2. Iterative feedback from design team
3. Final polish and optimization

### Tools & Software

**2D Art:**
- Photoshop, Illustrator (concept art, UI)
- Figma (UI/UX design and prototyping)

**3D Art:**
- Blender or Maya (modeling, rigging, animation)
- Substance Painter (texturing)
- ZBrush (high-detail sculpting for cinematics)

**Game Engine:**
- Unity (primary engine)
- Unity Shader Graph (custom materials)
- Unity VFX Graph (particle effects)

**Audio:**
- Pro Tools, Logic Pro, FL Studio (music composition)
- Audacity, Audition (sound design)

**Version Control:**
- Git + Git LFS for large binary files (art assets)
- Perforce (alternative for large game projects, better with binaries)

## Quality Assurance

### Art QA Checklist

- [ ] Consistent art style across all assets
- [ ] Performance-optimized (poly counts, texture sizes)
- [ ] Proper LODs for all 3D models
- [ ] Readable at gameplay camera distance
- [ ] Colorblind-friendly (UI and important gameplay elements)
- [ ] Culturally sensitive depictions (reviewed by consultants)

### Audio QA Checklist

- [ ] Balanced audio levels (no clipping, consistent volumes)
- [ ] Loopable music tracks (no awkward transitions)
- [ ] SFX synchronized with animations
- [ ] Spatial audio for 3D sounds (unit positions)
- [ ] Accessibility: visual indicators for audio cues (for deaf/hard-of-hearing players)

### Narrative QA Checklist

- [ ] Lore consistency (no contradictions)
- [ ] Proofread for grammar and spelling
- [ ] Cultural sensitivity review complete
- [ ] Clear and understandable for target audience
- [ ] Engaging and immersive (playtester feedback)

## Asset Management

### Naming Conventions

```
<AssetType>_<Faction>_<Name>_<Variant>_<LOD>

Examples:
Building_Yahud_Synagogue_Tier1_LOD0.fbx
Unit_Kristan_Crusader_Idle_Anim.fbx
VFX_Issam_Sandstorm_Particle.prefab
UI_Icon_Resource_Faith.png
```

### File Organization

```
/Assets
  /Art
    /Buildings
      /Yahud
      /Kristan
      /Issam
    /Units
    /Environment
    /UI
    /VFX
  /Audio
    /Music
    /SFX
    /VO
  /Narrative
    /Lore
    /Dialogue
    /Events
```

### Version Control Best Practices

- Commit early and often (daily check-ins)
- Descriptive commit messages ("Added Tier 2 Yahud temple model")
- Use branches for experimental work
- Regular backups (automated to cloud storage)
- Asset locks to prevent simultaneous editing of large files
